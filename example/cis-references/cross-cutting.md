## Practical reading order

1. Start with the OS family page to identify the current active version.
2. Use the dated CIS update posts to reconstruct the recent 2025/2026 release trail.
3. Treat WorkBench as the archive and member-download surface for older or alternative formats.

## General observations from the current public CIS pages

- CIS exposes active OS benchmark versions publicly on benchmark family pages.
- Older versions are not listed on the public family pages once they are no longer active; the family pages point to WorkBench for archived material.
- CIS uses two public article surfaces:
  - `www.cisecurity.org/insights/blog/...`
  - `portal.cisecurity.org/insights/articles/...`
- For this pack, both surfaces are treated as official CIS sources.

## Cross-platform operating-system picture as of 2026-04-23

- Windows is in a mixed 2025/2026 state: Windows 10 remains active in `4.0.0`, while Windows 11 Enterprise has advanced to `5.0.1`.
- Android currently exposes a single active Google Android benchmark line: `1.6.0`.
- iOS and macOS both expose current 2026-generation benchmark entries (`iOS 26`, `macOS 26 Tahoe`) while continuing to list current-generation 2025-era versions (`iOS 18`, `macOS 15.0 Sequoia`).
